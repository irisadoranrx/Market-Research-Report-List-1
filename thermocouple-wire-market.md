Type T (copper-constantan) is suitable for low temperatures, while Type J (iron-constantan) has a wide range but oxidizes at high temperatures. Type E (chromel-constantan) offers high output and temperature capability. Type K (chromel-alumel) is widely used due to its versatility. Type N (Nicrosil-Nisil) offers better stability at high temperatures. Type S and R (platinum-rhodium) are designed for high-precision applications, usually in laboratories.</p></p>
