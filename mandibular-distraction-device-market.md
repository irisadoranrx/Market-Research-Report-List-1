<p><ul><li>Muti-vector Distraction</li><li>Bi-directional Distraction</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Mandibular Distraction Device market is categorized into multi-vector distraction and bi-directional distraction types. Multi-vector distraction allows for adjustments in multiple directions, enhancing bone growth and alignment in complex cases. In contrast, bi-directional distraction focuses on simultaneous movement in two opposing directions, facilitating effective elongated healing in simpler cases. Both methods are utilized to address congenital jaw deformities and optimize outcomes in craniofacial surgery, reflecting the advancements in orthopedic and reconstructive techniques for improved patient care.</p></p>
